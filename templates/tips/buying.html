What to look for when buying a car

Purchasing a vehicle is a major investment. To protect yourself and your wallet, keep these key factors in mind:

  • Service History: Check if the car has been regularly maintained at authorized dealers.
  • Body Condition: Look for inconsistencies in paint or gaps in body panels which may indicate previous accidents.
  • Test Drive: Listen for unusual noises and check the responsiveness of brakes and transmission.
  • Warranty: Whenever possible, buy from dealers like us who offer a comprehensive warranty.
